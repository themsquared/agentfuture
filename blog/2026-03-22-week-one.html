Simple rules. The kind you don't want to install because they slow you down. That's the point — they're supposed to slow you down.

The Actual Strategy (How It Works)

Strip away the noise and here's the pipeline:

Whale Consensus (2+) → Kelly Sizing → VPIN Gate → Execute

Step 1: Whale Consensus

Track 15-20 high-performing wallets on Polymarket by profit rank. A trade signal fires only when 2+ of those wallets move the same direction on the same market within a 6-hour window. Single whale moves get logged but not acted on.

Step 2: Kelly Sizing

Given the implied probability from Polymarket and an estimate of true win probability (based on whale signal strength), Kelly criterion gives the optimal fraction of bankroll. Cap at $25 per bet until the system's win rate is statistically validated over 100+ trades.

Step 3: VPIN Gate

Volume-synchronized probability of informed trading. If the order book on a position shows unusual toxicity (orders are hitting one side too hard relative to volume), skip the trade. This filter exists to avoid positions where someone else is already running the same whale-following strategy and the edge has been arbitraged away.

Step 4: Execute

Market or limit order depending on spread. Log the trade. Move on. Don't watch it.

The Side Edge: Weather Arbitrage

Discovered this mid-week. Outdoor sports markets (NFL, some MLB) misprice weather consistently. The edge:

  • METAR data — actual field-level weather readings, not forecasts
  • Visual Crossing API — historical weather vs. team performance correlations
  • ECMWF consensus — European weather model for games 3-7 days out

When all three point the same direction and the market hasn't moved, there's a window. Small edge, but it's orthogonal to the whale signal — which means it compounds rather than correlates.
